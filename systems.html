🔬 The Science Behind The Protocol

Peer-reviewed research supporting our metabolic approach

Cortisol & Metabolic Dysfunction

Chronic stress elevates cortisol levels, which promotes abdominal fat storage, increases appetite, and disrupts metabolic function. Studies show cortisol is directly linked to metabolic syndrome.

Metabolic Adaptation to Dieting

Restrictive calorie diets trigger adaptive thermogenesis — your body compensates for calorie reduction by burning fewer calories. This is why weight loss plateaus and rebounds occur.

📄 View Study on PubMed →

Mineral Deficiencies & Metabolism

Magnesium and potassium deficiencies are strongly linked to insulin resistance and metabolic dysfunction. Supplementing these minerals supports healthy glucose metabolism.

📄 View Study on PubMed →

Sleep & Metabolic Health

Poor sleep disrupts leptin and ghrelin hormones, increasing hunger and reducing satiety signals. Quality sleep is essential for maintaining a healthy metabolism.

📄 View Study on PubMed →

Protein & Satiety

Higher protein intake increases satiety hormones and reduces overall calorie consumption. Protein also has the highest thermic effect of all macronutrients.

📄 View Study on PubMed →

Gut Microbiome & Weight

Gut bacteria composition influences nutrient absorption, inflammation, and metabolic rate. A healthy microbiome supports healthy weight management.
