The Hidden Problem: Why You're Always Tired

Most people blame their fatigue on lack of sleep, stress, or aging. But here's what many don't realize: your mitochondria — the tiny power plants inside your cells — may not be producing energy efficiently.

🔬 The Mitochondrial Connection

Mitochondria are responsible for producing ATP (adenosine triphosphate) — the energy currency that powers every function in your body. When mitochondrial function declines, you experience:

  • Persistent fatigue and low energy
  • Difficulty losing weight
  • Brain fog and poor concentration
  • Slower exercise recovery
  • Premature aging signs

As we age, mitochondrial function naturally declines by up to 50%. This is why you felt more energetic in your 20s than you do now.
