What Are Mitochondria?

Mitochondria are tiny organelles inside your cells that produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate) — the energy currency your body uses for everything from thinking to burning fat.

Each cell in your body contains hundreds to thousands of mitochondria. Muscle cells have the most (up to 4,000 per cell), which is why muscle burns more calories than fat.

When your mitochondria are functioning optimally, they burn fat efficiently. When they're damaged or sluggish, your metabolism slows down and fat storage increases.

🔬 The Science

Research shows that overweight people have fewer mitochondria and those mitochondria produce less ATP. By increasing mitochondrial density and function, you can increase your metabolic rate by up to 30%.

The Modern Energy Crisis

Our modern lifestyle is destroying our mitochondria. Processed foods, environmental toxins, chronic stress, and poor sleep all damage these cellular power plants.

When mitochondria become damaged, they produce less energy and more harmful free radicals. This creates a vicious cycle of fatigue, weight gain, and accelerated aging.

  • 🧠
    Brain Fog Your brain uses 20% of your energy — it feels the deficit first
  • 👵
